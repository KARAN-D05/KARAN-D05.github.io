I explore intelligence, thought, language and machinery through the lens of computation - building low-level systems, stored-program architectures, and hardware-level learning models. My work investigates how simple logical components combine to form memory, control, and adaptive behavior. I am particularly drawn to questions at the boundary of architecture and intelligence - how physical systems can implement abstraction, adaptation, and decision-making.

This site is record of that attempt - to understand how structure gives rise to thought.
