I build systems from first principles to understand how structure gives rise to computation.

I explore intelligence, thought, language and machinery through the lens of computation, How simple logical components combine to form memory, control and eventually learning - building low-level systems, stored-program architectures, and hardware-level learning models. I am drawn to questions at the boundary of structure and intelligence: how physical systems can implement abstraction, adaptation, and decision-making.

This site is a record of that attempt - to understand how structure gives rise to computation.
