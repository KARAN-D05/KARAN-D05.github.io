Karan Diwan

Interested in computation - from its mechanical beginnings to modern AI
Dovere is my hand, tho small, May I not add with truth; I do my best to please you all, Encourage then my youth. - Maillardet Automaton

I am interested in computation as machinery, intelligence and language - how simple logical components combine to form memory, control, and eventually learning.

My work explores computation from the ground up: building low-level computational machines, stored-program architectures, and hardware-level learning models to understand how structure gives rise to behavior.

I am particularly drawn to questions at the boundary of architecture and intelligence - how physical systems can implement abstraction, adaptation, and decision-making.

This site is a record of that exploration.
