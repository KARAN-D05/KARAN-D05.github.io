Karan Diwan

I am interested in computation as machinery, intelligence and language - how simple logical components combine to form memory, control, and eventually learning.

My work explores computation from the ground up: building arithmetic machines, stored-program architectures, and hardware-level learning models to understand how structure gives rise to behavior.

I am particularly drawn to questions at the boundary of architecture and intelligence - how physical systems can implement abstraction, adaptation, and decision-making.

This site is a record of that exploration.
